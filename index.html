About

A research profile built with the clarity of a product page.

I am a PhD student in computer science with research interests in information retrieval, natural language processing, and large language models. I care about building systems that are not only powerful, but also useful, reliable, and grounded in practical research questions.

My work explores how retrieval and language modeling can be combined to support better search, reasoning, and scientific workflows. This page is intentionally simple: one place for my background, my current directions, and the links that matter.

Current Interests

Retrieval-augmented language systems.

Designing pipelines that connect search, ranking, and language generation in a coherent and trustworthy way.

What I Value

Useful methods over ornamental complexity.

I am especially interested in evaluation, interpretability, and research outcomes that translate into usable tools and systems.

Research

Three directions I am actively building.

01

Information Retrieval

Improving search, ranking, and retrieval pipelines so users can access relevant evidence more precisely and efficiently.

02

Natural Language Processing

Studying how text understanding and generation methods can better support real-world language tasks and academic workflows.

03

Large Language Models

Exploring LLM-centered systems with a focus on robustness, evaluation, and reliable knowledge use in downstream applications.
